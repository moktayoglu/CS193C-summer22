Melis Oktayoglu

I am a senior undergraduate student double majoring in molecular biology and genetics, and computer engineering at Koc University, Turkey. I am at Stanford as an International Honors Program student for this summer session. I am currently interested in using machine learning to come up with new drug and antibody structures. Before coming to Stanford, I was working as an undergraduate researcher in Karaca Lab working for a machine learning model for predicting interacting pairs during molecular dynamics simulations. For the same group I also worked in a benchmarking study of common binding-energy change predictors. In that project, I employed a voting procedure among the predictors, and did various analysis on the binding energy enhancing and depleting mutation cases.

Prior to that, I've interned at Optofil where I helped developing a deep learning based bench-top automated cell counter. Also, during the pandemic I was co-authoring a review article on the bio-printed gliomablastoma models. I also tutored for multiple classes in my university which were Cell Biology and Development, MATLAB and Java courses. For the Java course I was a section leader for problem sessions. This was adopted from our university from the section leadings done for CS106 here at Stanford.
